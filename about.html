About PerkSnaps

Hello! I’m Ron Asnahon, and together with my best bud Carl John Arpay, we made PerkSnaps! We’re two fresh grads from the University of Cebu Lapu-Lapu and Mandaue SHS, ICT strand, and this site is our way of making Roblox codes less of a pain. We just graduated this May 2025, and we’re hyped to dive into this project. PerkSnaps is all about getting you those working codes for pets, coins, or whatever, without the hassle. It’s really really cool, and we’re pumped to tell you how it all happened!

How It Went Down

Okay, so it’s May 2025, and Carl and I are like, “We gotta make a website!” We just graduated, we’re free from high school, and we’re itching to build something dope with our ICT skills. But, man, we were totally out of ideas. We spent hours—HOURS—brainstorming, just chilling at my place, throwing around random thoughts. Nothing was clicking. Then, on May 26, we’re messing around on some Roblox code sites, and we notice something: the codes are all over the place! Like, some work, some don’t, and it’s super inconsistent. We looked at each other and were like, “Bruh, we can fix this!”

That was the spark for PerkSnaps. We decided to make a site where players can trust the codes to actually work. No more wasting time on expired stuff. We started coding right away, even though we were still figuring out what we were doing. It’s been a wild ride, but seeing you guys use the site makes it so worth it. We’re just getting started, and we’re hyped to keep improving it!

Making PerkSnaps

Building PerkSnaps was a total adventure. We’re not rich, so we used free tools we learned about in ICT class or from YouTube. Here’s how we made it happen after that May 26 lightbulb moment.

  • What We Used: I picked Node.js for the backend because it’s free and kinda easy once you get it. We use a free MongoDB to store the codes. Carl went with React.js to make the site fast and Tailwind CSS to make it look slick. We’re hosting it on some cheap server we found online, just enough to keep it running.
  • Fixing the Codes: The whole point was to make codes consistent. I made scripts to check Roblox games and developer posts on X. They’re not perfect—some codes still slip through—but we’re working on it. Carl added a button so you can tell us if a code’s bunk.
  • Easy for Everyone: Carl wanted the site to work for all players, so we added things like keyboard controls and a mode for screen readers. We learned about accessibility in school, but we’re still figuring it out. The light/dark mode is because we hate bright screens at night.
  • Figuring It Out: We started this right after graduation, so we’re learning as we go. Sometimes the site gets slow when a big game drops new codes, but I’m tweaking it to handle more people. Carl’s always testing it on his phone to make sure it’s smooth.

We’re still new at this, but we’re pouring our hearts into PerkSnaps. If you’ve got ideas, hit us up!
